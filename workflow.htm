Steps to processing proposal requests (Workflow)

Most grant-makers have a clear set of steps (workflow) to process proposals and each grant-maker differs from another.

Devman's project workflow is easily customised to your specific needs.

A typical workflow may be:

Workflow description

Status

Capture organisation and primary contact person's details

n/a

Capture proposal application details (sufficient details to and facts to understand the request)

proposal request

If decision is to immediately decline you may capture as a quick decline

decline

Gather more information about the candidate organisation and facts about the project such as:-

- detailed description   - conflict of interest    - budget     - recommendation     - grant conditions

This process culminates in the printing of a "board pack" to be presented to each member of a panel

under consideration

Post meeting decision to decline - a regret letter is sent

decline

Post meeting decision to approve. The grantee is informed by grant letter and grant contract (MOU)

Many organisations will pledge the tranches over a period of time and this may be contingent on receipt of a progress report

approved

Your organisation continues to interact with grantee  until completed

approved

The project is concluded and all payments have been made

completed

       Outline of steps to manage proposals in DevMan

1 Managing projects and proposals

Proposals go through a work-flow cycle

The basic steps for handling proposals received.

•Add (capture) a proposal

•Print an acknowledgment letter

•Change a project status (decline/approve)

•Print an acceptance/approval and grant agreement

•Add a pledge to an approved project

•Make a payment for an approved project

•Complete a project

•You may want to see dashboard displays, print project reports and export to Excel.
