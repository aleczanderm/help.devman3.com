Alumni roll-out

You’ve upgraded to Devman to enhance productivity and manage your large Alumni data.

Will it benefit you as promised? Will it be easier, quicker and will it automate tasks you had to do manually?

Yes - and more - but to see the benefits of this upgrade you need a successful roll-out/training strategy.

This training will help you do your work not simply as before but to do it more effectively, quickly and easier than with the old methods – and it’s designed to get you there as quickly as possible.

Moving to new software can be a challenge – be patient! Right now, because you know your older system well, you may be able to do things easily and quickly. Be realistic! Devman is a powerful sophisticated system and will take time to master.  Rather than throw you in the deep end we strongly encourage a phased approach with timeframes to accomplish tasks.

A few more important factors to take into account are:

▪What is your role with Devman  (casual or full-time user)

▪You and the team’s skill level

▪Appropriate method of  training (seminar, individual and class-room)

 

We discuss all these factors with you and tailor the roll-out accordingly.

1 - System setup and data imports

Your Devman system will be of high value when it has a comprehensive and accurate set of alumni data.

Therefore acquisition of quality data from legacy system is imperative and must precede training. Steps and responsibilities include:-

-Devman will provide you with an import specification (layout)

-Client obtains the official alumni data from your student system (Peoplesoft, ITS, Oracle or other system)

-Devman will provide us with other alumni data such as mailing lists or spreadsheets

-Devman will inspect both sets of data and import into Devman

-We will both check the data imported correctly and accurately (batch totals and random visual checks).
This will include viewing in Devman via reports and dashboards.
2 - Training - Basic data management

After a lively demonstration of Devman’s powerful features there will be hands-on basic training. This will cover how to:

-View and update alumni profiles (contact details and their qualifications).

-Search (or filter) on a category or group of alumni (e.g. by faculty, qualification, graduation year and/or demographics)

-Produce a list or summary report and/or a customised export.

-Communicate (via text message or email) to one person or a large group (mailshot)
3 - Ongoing data management and what's next

A few more important factors and other features that will pay dividends:

- Look after your alumni database - keep it up-to-date.

- Have a  portal on your website and invite your alumni to update their own details.

- Communicate with your alumni - send newsletters in a mailshot.

- Invite them to special events with Event management

- Manage alumni subscriptions
