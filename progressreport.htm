Progress reports


Contents

Whether you're managing a project as a grant-maker or fundraiser Devman provides the needed tools for Project Progress Report (PPR) management.

 

The purpose of the PPR is to provide stakeholders with a summary of the project status at regular intervals.

The report puts together financial and substantive data to reflect project achievements with the project plans and monitor project progress.

It can include information on risks and issues and thus advise concerned parties of any potential problem or areas where help can be given.

Project Progress Reports serve also as inputs for the monitoring of corresponding outcomes.

 

As a recipient (grantee) of a successful (approved/funded) project, you are expected to update the donor on the progress of your project.  

As a donor (grant-maker) you are concerned that the funds are used correctly and will therefore demand one or more progress reports before releasing the tranche.

 

In many cases the donor or organisation will have specific requirements that must be adhered to for a progress report.

If not you will want to make your report easy to ready and create a standard - here are some considerations:-

 Add a Project Progress Report (Grant-making)

Grant-making:

There are a few things to consider:

1.Setting up an automated trigger to remind someone to get a progress report from the grantee.
This is done by adding workflow on the project e.g.:
a) Interim report triggered when the project status is changed to "approved"  at 180 days from approval date.
b) Final report triggered when the project status is changed to "completed" at 360 days from completion date.
 

2.Adding the received PPR from the grantee into the project.
When the PPR is received it is scanned and attached to the progress report activity.
 

3.Checking the PPR and deciding it is satisfactory or not
The programme officer check if the the criteria has been met and records this decision.
 

4.Then releasing or withholding the next tranche.
The next tranche is "released" and passed onto finance who are authorised to pay the project.
