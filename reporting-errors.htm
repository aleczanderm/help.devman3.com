How do I report an error effectively?

Errors are fixed more quickly and more thoroughly with your cooperation and the information you give the DevMan developers.

Give clear, helpful and informative information so that the developer involved can see what's wrong in front of them.

Since you can't always show them in person give them careful and  detailed instructions on how to reproduce the error.

If they can see it for themselves they will gather extra information until they know the cause. If they can't they will ask you to gather that information for them.

 

What should you do when something unexpected happens?

Carefully take note of what you see that is out of the ordinary.

If possible, take a screenshot and paste it with your report.  

First give the symptoms and what you think will fix the problem - make sure the developers know that too.

 

Faults may seem to be intermittent, but this may not be so - the sequence of events leading up to them may be complex.

They may happen only under some not so obvious conditions such as:

•time related - for example ok in the morning but not after lunch

•environmental - the variations are endless ... screen resolution, memory, laptop vs desktop, browser versions.

The developer will need to investigate and ask you a lot of questions to determine if the problem really is intermittent.

Patience may be needed - you may be asked to try on a colleagues pc or go through it step by step with the developer.

 

Please also refer to our DevMan Help FAQ section as it may shed light on your problem.

 

How do I follow up after I have reported an error?

To ensure that your problem is resolved, please answer additional questions when asked.

You need to continue to help fixers resolve your bug - a lack of information may mean it stays unresolved.

Take the time to give more information and try the solution proposed. Communication is key.

In time the fixer will be up to speed with you and you will both understand each other.

If you have solved it on your own please let us know and see it closed rather than leave us looking for a solution already found.

 

Some do's and dont's when reporting a bug

Don't report a new bug unless you are sure it hasn't been reported before.

Please be patient - Developers will likely ask you for more details and give you some things to try. Without continued feedback, bugs cannot quickly be closed.

Be descriptive and concise. Report what your specific problem is.

 

For example - "Tax certificates batch generation fails at print phase" rather than "DevMan is not working".

Severity:

•Critical failure affects all DevMan users. They cannot do anything and there's no apparent workaround.

•High key function does not work and there's no easy workaround. This impacts many users.

•Medium a non essential functionality does not work or program crashes at that point.

•Low optional functionality does not work.

•Very low documentation mistake - something that really does not matter much but should be noted for future release.

User story:

Include relevant information depending on what your problem is.
Indicate whether the function worked before and if so when you think it stopped.

 

Next Step:

Person assigned to take on the problem

 

Comment:

Explain how to reproduce the bug if you can, is important. This will help when testing the bug and make the turnaround much quicker.
Trace your steps i.e. list of the things you did leading up to the bug especially if DevMan crashes.
