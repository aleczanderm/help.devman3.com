Including linked and embedded images


Contents

 

Development Manager allows for linked images to be included in the body of emails / templates. These images are not actually sent with the email. Instead, when you compose the email the images are uploaded to the Development Manager server and a placeholder is placed in the body of the email. When the message is received by the recipients, their email clients “fetch” the images from the Development Manager server and display them correctly in the body of the email. (For more detailed information on this see the KidzAfrica Email Best Practice document.)  Embedded images however are sent with the email and are always displayed.

 

1.In Full screen mode, place the cursor where you want the linked image to be placed and click on the Insert Image button on the toolbar. This opens the Image Properties webpage dialog.

2.To upload an image click on the Browse server button, search for and select the image file that you wish to upload, click Open.

 

Click here to view

 

3.Click the Upload button to transfer the image to the Development Manager server. When this action is completed you will see the image name in the Files section of the Modify image dialog. Click on the image file name.

 

Click here to view

 

4.The Preview section shows you a preview of the image you have uploaded. You can change the properties of the image as well as add Alternative text (a message that displays when the mouse cursor is placed over the image). You can change the size of the image by placing values in the height and width controls.

 

Click here to view

 

5.To embed an image, when you insert an image into an email template, in the insert/edit image dialog simply add an asterisk (*) in the Alternative Text field before the text as in the screenshot below.

6.Once you've made your changes, click on the OK button. You will be returned to the Full screen editing window where you will now see the image you have uploaded and included in the body of your email.

7.To make any further modifications, you can select it and click on the Insert Image button again to modify its properties. You can also click-and-drag the image to the desired place in the email body.
